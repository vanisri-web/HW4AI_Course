## Project: Spiking Neural Network Speedup Chip

### What are you trying to do?
Create a tiny chip that makes brain-like pattern recognition networks run much faster on sensors and robots, using very little battery power.

### How is it done today, and what are the limits of current practice?
Small computer chips today simulate these brain-like networks slowly—one decision takes milliseconds. Robots react too late, and batteries drain fast from non-stop calculations.

### What is new in your approach and why do you think it will be successful?
This chip handles events directly like real brains, skipping slow step-by-step math. It will work because real brains process sparse signals efficiently—we copy that in hardware for instant, low-power results.
